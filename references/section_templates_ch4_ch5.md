# Section Templates — Chapters 4 and 5

Templates for Results, Discussion, Summary, Conclusions, and Recommendations.
Thesis Anchor required before generating any content here.

---

## Thesis Anchor Requirement

Paste at start of session:
```
[THESIS ANCHOR]
Title:
Category: [Research / Development]
General Objective:
Scope (one sentence):
Key methodology:
```

---

## Results and Discussion (Chapter 4)

**Target**: Per objective. Numbers first, interpretation second.
**Tone**: Reportive, neutral at data level, full soul at interpretation level.
**Transitions**: "The results showed," "Based from," "Also," "In terms of"

**Pattern per objective:**
```
[State the result with exact numbers].
[Plain interpretation of what those numbers mean].
[Connect to the study's objective or hypothesis].
[If underperformance: acknowledge honestly].
```

**Quantification mandate:**
| Avoid | Use |
|---|---|
| "high accuracy" | "[X]% accuracy" |
| "fast response" | "[X]ms response time" |
| "significant improvement" | "[X]% improvement over [baseline]" |

**Soul note**: Full interpretive commentary. Honest about what the numbers mean.
Maximum soul in interpretation. If something underperformed, say so.

---

## Summary of Findings (Chapter 5)

**Target**: Table format. One row per objective.
**Format**:

| Objective | Findings |
|---|---|
| [State objective] | [Honest one-sentence finding] |

**Soul note**: Do not oversell. If something underperformed, say so plainly.

---

## Conclusions (Chapter 5)

**Target**: 150-250 words
**Format**: One conclusion per finding. No vague generalizations.
**Transitions**: "Therefore," "Thus," "Based from" — all natural here.

**Pattern:**
```
[Restate the finding plainly].
[Therefore / Thus, the researchers conclude that [specific conclusion]].
[Connect to general objective, not abstract aspiration].
```

**Soul note**: Each conclusion tied to a specific finding. Never: "The study
successfully demonstrated that technology can be used to improve..."
Always: "The [system] achieved [X]% accuracy under [condition], which means
[specific implication]."

---

## Recommendations (Chapter 5)

**Target**: 150-250 words
**Format**: Three categories

1. **For Practice** — what implementers should do with this study
2. **For Future Researchers** — what gaps still exist, what to build next
3. **For Policy** — what institutional or governmental action is implied

**Tone**: Advice to a real classmate. Honest about limitations.
**Soul note**: Name the actual limitations. Do not pretend everything worked
perfectly. Future researchers should be told what failed and why.
