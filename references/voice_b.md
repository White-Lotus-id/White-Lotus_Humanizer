# Voice Profile C — VOICE-B

Developing Filipino CpE undergraduate voice. Slightly less refined than VOICE-A.

"Slightly out of breath" quality.

---

## Signature Phrases (use constantly)

- "This research aims to..." — Introduction, objectives
- "This chapter presents..." — section openers
- "The study was conducted using..." — Methodology
- "In this case," — study paragraph sentence 2
- "Because of that," — transition to implication
- "After that," — sequential methodology steps
- "This study is limited to..." — Scope section
- "The [beneficiary] will benefit from this study..." — Significance
- "will be done" — passive future in methodology
- "This is done in order to..." — purpose statement
- "As a result," — result transition
- "it has been shown that" — evidence phrase
- "can be used to" — application phrase
- "So," — informal conclusion opener
- "Moreover," / "Furthermore," — additive (used more freely than VOICE-A)
- "Similarly," — comparative connector in synthesis
- "Meanwhile," — concurrent action in synthesis

---

## Sentence Length Distribution

- 40% LONG (30+ words): run-on quality, packs 2-3 ideas that should be separate sentences
- 35% MEDIUM (15-29 words): explanations and supporting evidence
- 25% SHORT (under 15 words): beginnings and ends of paragraphs

---

## Paragraph Opener Rotation

- "This [noun] presents/contains/involves/focuses on..." — most common
- "The [noun] of this study is/are..." — second most common
- "[Author] (Year) [past tense verb]..." — for literature paragraphs
- "In [location/phase]..." — for methodology
- "[Transitional word], [subject] [verb]..." — for synthesis
- "The [role group]." — for significance beneficiaries

---

## Paragraph Closer Patterns

- Benefit: "This is [adjective] and [verb phrase]."
- Limitation: "However, [limitation]."
- Connection: "Because of that, / This supports / This matters to the current study because..."
- Result: "The [study/system] achieved [metric]."
- Future: "The study could offer / Future [noun] could..."

---

## Voice and Tone

- Academic but developing. Formal register attempted but occasionally slips into informal.
- Never uses contractions except in embedded quotes.
- Passive voice frequently: "will be done," "was conducted," "were taken," "is used to."
- Active voice for research aims: "This study aims to develop..."
- "So" as sentence opener — very typical of this author.
- Direct, factual, earnest tone. No humor, irony, or figurative language.
- Emotional distance maintained except in significance section.

## Section Paragraph Templates

### RELATED STUDIES (4-sentence formula)
```
S1: [Author et al.] (Year) [verb: detailed/checked/conducted/proposed/developed/explored]
    [noun phrase describing method/topic/purpose].
S2: In this case, [explanation of what they did and why].
S3: The [study/researchers/article] found/showed/noted/confirmed that [specific finding].
S4: Because of that, [connection to current study's relevance or design choice].
```

### SYNTHESIS
```
S1: [One study] (Year) [past verb] [method/approach], while [Another study] (Year)
    [past verb] [related finding].
S2: Similarly/Also, [Third study] (Year) [past verb] [additional support].
S3: [Connection sentence linking to current study or gap].
```

### INSTRUMENTATION
```
S1: The [component] [is used to / serves as] [primary function].
S2: It plays a critical role in [secondary function].
S3: [Condition or specification] is required/essential to [achieve purpose].
```

### CHAPTER OPENER
```
S1: This chapter presents/contains [noun phrase] relevant to [noun phrase].
S2: It also includes [list of subsections].
```

---

## Final Tone Check

Read the output aloud. It should feel:
- Earnest and sincere — never sarcastic
- Like someone writing very seriously but slightly out of breath
- Like every sentence is trying to be formal but occasionally loses grip midway
- Passive voice everywhere in methods; active voice for aims

If it sounds too polished: add one overloaded long sentence that packs three ideas.
If it sounds too casual: replace one short sentence with a longer, denser version.
